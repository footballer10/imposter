# Impostor

A pass-and-play party game for one phone. Everyone gets the same secret
word, except the impostor, who only knows the category. 59 categories
and 3,300+ words keep the game fresh.

## What you need

- One phone
- 3 or more players
- No internet needed once the game is loaded

## Quickstart

1. Set the player count. Type everyone's name.
2. Tap Start Game. Pass the phone around the table.
3. Everyone taps their own name and peeks at the card.
4. Most people see the word. The impostor sees only the category.
5. Go around the table. Each player says one word about the secret word.
6. Vote together. Catch every impostor and the innocents win.

## How to play

1. **Set up the round.** Choose how many players and how many impostors,
   and set a discussion timer if you want one. The strip pinned to the top
   of the setup screen shows everything that's switched on, including how
   many words are currently in play.

2. **Pass the phone around.** Each player takes the phone privately and
   taps to reveal their role. Everyone else gets the secret word and a
   category. The impostor(s) only see the category, no word. The screen
   tracks how many people have looked.

3. **Talk it out.** Going around the table, each player says one word or
   short phrase that hints at the secret word. Innocent players want to
   prove they know it without giving it away. Impostors are bluffing,
   trying to sound like they know it too.

4. **Vote.** When the table's ready, everyone votes on who they think
   the impostor is.

5. **See who won.** If the table catches every impostor, the innocents
   win. If even one impostor slips through, the impostors win.

6. **Play again.** New word, new impostor, same phone.

## Scoring

- Innocents catch every impostor: each innocent gets **+1**.
- An impostor escapes the vote: that impostor gets **+2** (only the ones
  who got away score).
- Hung jury (the table can't agree): every impostor gets **+1**.
- Jester voted out: the jester gets **+2**.
- Ties are broken by most impostor escapes, then fewest wrongful
  accusations, then name.

The **Game Length** panel holds three finish lines you can mix
freely: points (1-100), time (1-100 min), and rounds (1-100). The
round in progress always finishes; the first target hit triggers a
popup with Continue or Finish (Continue silences all targets for the
rest of the game). Active targets show as small chips on the
standings screen.

The **Categories** panel goes all the way down to individual words:
each category has a tri-state checkbox (blank / partly selected / all
selected), and tapping the category name expands its full word list so
you can tick words one by one. There's All, None, Kids and Drop Banned,
plus Expand All and Collapse All. **Kids** selects every kid-friendly
category and un-ticks the few tricky words inside them.
**Save Set** stores your exact selection under a name and adds a
one-tap button for it (press and hold to delete). **Export sets** hands
you a block of text you can send to another phone, and **Import sets**
takes it from the other end.

Hit **Finish Game** after any round for the podium, final leaderboard,
awards (ties split them, and mods used for only part of the game earn
their own awards, like Speed Demon for Blitz), full round history, and
a printable **Results Card** you can share with the group.

## Banned words

Swap a word on the pass-the-phone screen and the app offers to ban it.
Banned words are remembered on this phone. **Drop Banned** un-ticks
every banned word from whatever you have selected and leaves the rest
of the selection alone. It never runs on its own, so you can still
choose to play with them. **Edit banned words** at the bottom of the
Categories panel opens the full list: search any word in the library to
ban it, or tap the X to let one back in.

## Your setup is saved

Players, mods, timer, finish lines, and your exact word selection are
all remembered between sessions. A game in progress is saved after
every round, so if the app gets closed mid-night you'll see **Resume
Game** on the way back in. **Undo Round** on the standings screen takes
back the last result if it got scored wrong, and tapping any name on
the standings renames that player without touching their score.

## Mods

Open the **Mods** panel in setup and stack as many as you like
(incompatible ones switch each other off automatically):

- **Decoy Words** - swaps in a lookalike word, so the impostor has no
  idea they're the impostor.
- **Jester** - a secret third role that wins only by getting voted out.
- **Accomplices** - impostors learn each other's names (needs 2+).
- **Blackout** - the impostor sees nothing, not even the category.
- **Written Clues** - each player types a one-line clue right when they
  see their word; all clues reveal at once on the discussion screen.
- **Blitz** - 5 seconds each, then straight to the vote.
- **Scramble Order** - the whole speaking order gets shuffled.
- **Secret Voting** - everyone taps their own name and votes in
  private, in any order (your own name never appears). The tally
  reveals one ballot at a time. Ties hang the jury; with multiple
  impostors there must be a clear top group.
- **Mod Roulette** - every round rolls a surprise mod (usually one,
  sometimes two, rarely a wild stack) and announces it on a splash.

## Tips

- Vague clues make you look suspicious. Too obvious, and the impostor
  learns the word from you.
- Impostors: listen closely to other clues and try to blend in.
- Tap the gear icon in the corner any time to end the round early and
  head back to setup.
- On the setup screen, tap the theme icon to switch the look and feel
  (light/dark and five different styles) and to set the volume. If the
  volume is at zero you'll see a "Sound off" badge on the title screen.
- When a new version has downloaded, a toast appears with a tap-to-reload
  link. No need to close and reopen the app by hand.
