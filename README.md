# Imposter

A pass-and-play party game for one phone. Everyone gets the same secret
word... except the imposter, who only knows the category. 59 categories
and 3,300+ words keep the game fresh.

## What you need

- One phone
- 3 or more players
- No internet needed once the game is loaded

## How to play

1. **Set up the round.** Choose how many players and how many imposters,
   and set a discussion timer if you want one.

2. **Pass the phone around.** Each player takes the phone privately and
   taps to reveal their role. Everyone else gets the secret word and a
   category. The imposter(s) only see the category — no word.

3. **Talk it out.** Going around the table, each player says one word or
   short phrase that hints at the secret word. Innocent players want to
   prove they know it without giving it away. Imposters are bluffing,
   trying to sound like they know it too.

4. **Vote.** When the table's ready, everyone votes on who they think
   the imposter is.

5. **See who won.** If the table catches every imposter, the innocents
   win. If even one imposter slips through, the imposters win.

6. **Play again.** New word, new imposter, same phone.

## Scoring

- Innocents catch every imposter: each innocent gets **+1**.
- An imposter escapes the vote: that imposter gets **+2** (only the ones
  who got away score).
- Hung jury (the table can't agree): every imposter gets **+1**.
- Jester voted out: the jester gets **+2**.
- Ties are broken by most imposter escapes, then fewest wrongful
  accusations, then name.

Hit **Finish Game** after any round for the podium, final leaderboard,
awards, and full round history.

## Mods

Open the **Mods** panel in setup and stack as many as you like
(incompatible ones switch each other off automatically):

- **Decoy Words** — the imposter gets a close-but-wrong word and has no
  idea they're the imposter.
- **Kid Mode** — only categories and words kids can connect to.
- **Jester** — a secret third role that wins only by getting voted out.
- **Accomplices** — imposters learn each other's names (needs 2+).
- **Blackout** — the imposter sees nothing, not even the category.
- **Written Clues** — everyone types a secret clue; all reveal at once.
- **Blitz** — 5 seconds each, then straight to the vote.
- **Scramble Order** — the whole speaking order gets shuffled.

## Tips

- Vague clues make you look suspicious. Too obvious, and the imposter
  learns the word from you.
- Imposters: listen closely to other clues and try to blend in.
- Tap the gear icon in the corner any time to end the round early and
  head back to setup.
- On the setup screen, tap the theme icon to switch the look and feel
  (light/dark and five different styles).
