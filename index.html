Quickstart

  1. Set the player count. Type everyone’s name.
  2. Tap Start Game. Pass the phone around the table.
  3. Everyone taps their own name and peeks at the card.
  4. Most people see the word. The impostor sees only the category.
  5. Go around the table. Each player says one word about the secret word.
  6. Vote together. Catch every impostor and the innocents win.

The Game

Everyone secretly gets the same word from a category. The impostor gets the category and nothing else. Take turns saying one word that hints at the secret word. Innocents want to prove they know it without giving it away. Impostors bluff and blend in. Then the table votes. Catch every impostor and the innocents win. Let one slip through and the impostors take it.

A Round

  • Pass the phone. Each player taps their name and privately reveals their role.
  • Clues. The timer screen names who starts. Go clockwise from there. Vague clues look guilty. Obvious clues teach the impostor the word.
  • Vote together. Pick exactly as many people as there are impostors. With one impostor, tapping a new name simply moves the vote.
  • Hung Jury. Table can’t agree? Every impostor escapes with 1 point.
  • Scrap Round. Someone blurted the word? Scrap it. No points, fresh word.
  • Swap the Word. On the pass-the-phone screen you can swap for a new word. Roles get re-dealt, and the app offers to ban the old word on the way out.

Scoring

  • Innocents catch every impostor → each innocent +1.
  • An impostor escapes the vote → that impostor +2 (only the ones who got away score).
  • Hung jury → each impostor +1.
  • Jester gets voted out → jester +2 (and any escaped impostors still get their +2).

Tiebreaker: equal points are settled by most impostor escapes (jester wins count), then fewest times wrongly voted out, then name.

The Mods

  • Decoy Words. The impostor gets a similar-but-wrong word and has no idea they’re the impostor. Their clue lands slightly off. Can’t combine with Blackout or Accomplices.
  • Jester. One extra secret role. The jester knows the word but only wins by getting voted out (+2). House rule: the jester may never point the vote at themselves. Needs at least 2 more players than impostors.
  • Accomplices. Impostors learn each other’s names. Needs 2+ impostors.
  • Blackout. The impostor sees nothing, not even the category. Pure bluff.
  • Written Clues. Each player types a one-line clue right when they see their word, and can’t pass the phone until they have. All clues reveal at once on the discussion screen. Can’t combine with Blitz or Scramble Order.
  • Blitz. Each player gets 5 seconds to say one word, in order, then straight to the vote. No discussion, ignores the timer setting.
  • Secret Voting. Everyone taps their own name and votes in private, in any order. You cast as many votes as there are impostors, and your own name never appears, so the jester can’t self-vote. The tally is revealed one ballot at a time. With one impostor, most votes is accused and a tie hangs the jury. With more, there has to be a clear top group of exactly that size. 5-4-4 with two impostors hangs the jury. 5-5-4 convicts both 5s.
  • Mod Roulette. Every round secretly rolls a surprise set of mods and announces them on a round splash you dismiss with a tap. Usually one, sometimes two, rarely a wild stack, never incompatible ones. Switching it on clears every manual mod. Roulette does the picking.

Speaking Order

By default a random player starts and the clue phase goes clockwise from them. Flip Scramble speaking order in setup to shuffle the whole order and print it on the timer screen instead. It has no effect with Written Clues, where everyone answers at once.

Game Length

The Game Length panel holds three finish lines you can mix freely: points (1–100), time (1–100 min), and rounds (1–100). The round in progress always finishes. The first target reached triggers a popup with Continue or Finish. Choosing Continue silences all targets for the rest of the game. Active targets show as small chips on the standings screen.

Categories & Words

The Categories panel goes all the way down to the word. Each category has a checkbox on the left. Blank means nothing from it is in play, colored-in means some words are, a check means all of them. Tapping the box selects everything, or clears everything when it’s already checked. Tap the category name to expand its full word list and tick words one by one. All and None tick or clear everything. Expand all opens every word list at once. Kids selects every kid-friendly category and quietly un-ticks the handful of tricky words inside them.

Save Set stores your exact selection under a name and adds a button next to Kids. Tap it any time to snap right back to that set. Sets are kept on your phone between sessions. Press and hold a set button to delete it. Export sets hands you a block of text you can send to another phone, and Import sets takes it from the other end.

Banned Words

Swap a word on the pass-the-phone screen and the app offers to ban it. Banned words are remembered on this phone. Drop Banned un-ticks every banned word from whatever you have selected and leaves the rest of the selection alone. It never runs on its own, so you can still choose to play with them. Edit banned words sits at the bottom of the Categories panel. Search any word in the library to ban it, or tap the X to let one back in.

Your Setup Is Saved

Players, mods, timer, finish lines, and your exact word selection are all remembered between sessions. A game in progress is saved after every round, so if the app gets closed mid-night you’ll see Resume Game on the way back in. Undo Round on the standings screen takes back the last result if it got scored wrong. Tap any name on the standings to rename that player without touching their score.

Results Card

On the Awards screen, Results Card builds a printable, shareable page with the podium, full standings, and every award from the night.

Sound

If you hear nothing, flip your phone’s ring/silent switch. iPhones mute game audio in silent mode. Volume lives in the theme menu on the setup screen.
