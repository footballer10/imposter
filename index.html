The Game

Everyone secretly gets the same word from a category — except the impostor, who only sees the category. Take turns saying one word that hints at the secret word. Innocents want to prove they know it without giving it away. Impostors bluff and blend in. Then the table votes. Catch every impostor and the innocents win; if even one slips through, the impostors take it.

A Round

  • Pass the phone. Each player taps their name and privately reveals their role.
  • Clues. The timer screen names who starts — then go clockwise around the table. Vague clues look guilty; obvious clues teach the impostor the word.
  • Vote together. Pick exactly as many people as there are impostors. With one impostor, tapping a new name simply moves the vote.
  • Hung Jury. Table can’t agree? Every impostor escapes with 1 point.
  • Scrap Round. Someone blurted the word? Scrap it — no points, fresh word.
  • Swap the Word. On the pass-the-phone screen you can swap for a new word (roles get re-dealt).

Scoring

  • Innocents catch every impostor → each innocent +1.
  • An impostor escapes the vote → that impostor +2 (only the ones who got away score).
  • Hung jury → each impostor +1.
  • Jester gets voted out → jester +2 (and any escaped impostors still get their +2).

Tiebreaker: equal points are settled by most impostor escapes (jester wins count), then fewest times wrongly voted out, then name.

The Mods

  • Decoy Words — the impostor gets a similar-but-wrong word and has no idea they’re the impostor. Their clue lands slightly off… Can’t combine with Blackout or Accomplices.
  • Jester — one extra secret role. The jester knows the word but only wins by getting voted out (+2). House rule: the jester may never point the vote at themselves. Needs at least 2 more players than impostors.
  • Accomplices — impostors learn each other’s names. Needs 2+ impostors.
  • Blackout — the impostor sees nothing, not even the category. Pure bluff.
  • Written Clues — each player types a one-line clue right when they see their word (you can’t pass the phone until you’ve typed one); all clues reveal at once on the discussion screen. Can’t combine with Blitz or Scramble Order.
  • Blitz — each player gets 5 seconds to say one word, in order, then straight to the vote. No discussion, ignores the timer setting.
  • Scramble Order — instead of clockwise-from-a-random-start, the entire speaking order is shuffled and shown.
  • Secret Voting — everyone taps their own name and votes in private, in any order (as many votes as there are impostors; your own name never appears, so the jester can’t self-vote). The tally is revealed one ballot at a time. One impostor: most votes is accused, a tie hangs the jury. Multiple: there must be a clear top group of exactly that size — 5-4-4 with two impostors hangs, 5-5-4 convicts both 5s.
  • Mod Roulette — every round secretly rolls a surprise set of mods (usually one, sometimes two, rarely a wild stack — never incompatible ones) and announces them on a round splash. Switching it on clears every manual mod — roulette does the picking.

Game Length

The Game Length panel holds three finish lines you can mix freely: points (1–100), time (1–100 min), and rounds (1–100). The round in progress always finishes; the first target reached triggers a popup with Continue or Finish. Choosing Continue silences all targets for the rest of the game. Active targets show as small chips on the standings screen — the point goal, minutes left, and rounds left.

Categories

The Categories panel goes down to the word. Each category has a checkbox on the left — blank means nothing from it is in play, colored-in means some words are, a check means all of them. Tapping the box selects everything (or clears everything when it’s already checked). Tap the category name to expand its full word list and tick words one by one. All and None tick or clear everything, Expand all opens every word list at once, and the Kids button selects every kid-friendly category and quietly un-ticks the handful of tricky words inside them.

Save Set stores your exact selection under a name and adds a button next to Kids — tap it any time to snap right back to that set. Sets are kept on your phone between sessions. Press and hold a set button to delete it.

Results Card

On the Awards screen, Results Card builds a printable, shareable page with the podium, full standings, and every award from the night.

Sound

If you hear nothing, flip your phone’s ring/silent switch — iPhones mute game audio in silent mode. You can also toggle sound off in the theme menu.
